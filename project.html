Thunkable App Project

Course: Computer Science Principles
Dates: September 2018 - October 2018


A screenshot of the app running on an iPhone

To begin with, I had an idea for a clicker type game where you earn points by rapidly clicking a button. After 3 days of work making screen backgrounds and attempts at lining up the assets on thunkable, I abandoned the project with two days left and started from scratch. Since I play the alto sax in the school marching band, I figured Iâ€™d play to my strengths (no pun intended) and make a digital instrument simulator. Since the alto sax is a long instrument I could only feasibly include a few of its keys as a sample size. I first programmed and added the keys, then focused on making them play and stop playing a sound when pressed and lifted. Then I made a loop that checks and updates text in realtime to display the name of the note being played for debugging sake. After it all seemed to be working well, I added the extra buttons for sharp notes and flat notes, added a description at the top of the screen, and it was ready to go.
